Flexi Module- The art of trying to dance to the music

The Flexi module gave me a chance to express my creativity in the form of dance, music, and the visual arts. I began my week with the dance module, trying to get a group of teenagers to willingly dance in front of their peers was quite the feat that our teachers pulled of, even after initially being reluctant, most of us were enthusiastically participating in the activity. We were introduced to the dance style Bhangra, a type of Indian folk dance and we learned a 30-second choreography that was fun and easy to remember and in the coming weeks, all those who have chosen the dance module will have a blast learning about new dance styles. After the dance module came the music module, the music modules will allow to experience and experiment with music file editing on a basic software called Audacity, they will try their hand at experimenting with music composition with Garage band or Audacity and they will learn to read and compose basic rhythm patterns.

The music module paves the way for their entry into IBDP music as well as they will learn skills so necessary in this ever changing landscape, of course we didn’t get the chance to dive into the fun stuff in our first class instead we spent the session answering the question of how music affects our day to day life, it was incredible to see how big of an impact music has had on all our lives and how it helps us, everyone answered that they listened to music almost every day. Although we ran out of time in our session for anyone curious about what taking music as a subject is like can read Rohan Gulati's experience as a music hl student:

IB Music HL has been a rollercoaster! Despite it being one of the least scoring subjects in IB, I chose it as one of my HL subjects because I wanted to explore music at a level I had never experienced, and I think that’s exactly what I’ve done after taking the subject. I have experimented to learn new techniques and concepts which I never thought I would do in my life, for example, learning how to create multiple genres of music through music technology and software! And last but certainly not least we have the visual arts module, when we arrived at our class we were greeted with blank papers with prompts written on a post on the wall. We were given an hour to pick any prompt and express what it meant to us. It was an incredible experience to sit in silence with our peers for an hour and deliberate what these different questions meant to us, it was also quite comical!
